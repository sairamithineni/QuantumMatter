About

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist with a research focus on complex oxide heterostructures, frustrated magnetic lattices, and topological semimetal systems. My work investigates emergent quantum phenomena arising from strong electronic correlations and symmetry-driven effects, with particular emphasis on magnetotransport and spin-dependent transport mechanisms.

Experimentally, I specialize in thin-film synthesis, structural and electronic characterization, and low-temperature magnetotransport measurements. Through interface engineering and symmetry control, I aim to realize and manipulate novel quantum phases, bridging fundamental condensed matter physics with future spintronic and quantum electronic technologies.

Physical Review B

Influence of Kondo effect on Spin Chirality induced GHE in Weakly frustrated Ruthenium Pyrochlore

The spin-chirality-induced geometrical Hall effect (GHE) originates from a real-space Berry phase acquired by itinerant electrons traversing noncoplanar spin textures with finite scalar chirality, producing an emergent magnetic field.

Interfacial Octahedral Distortion Induced Weyl Semimetallicity and Anomalous Hall Effect in SrNbO₃/LaFeO₃ Bilayers

The Dirac semimetallic state in SrNbO₃/SrTiO₃ thin films is stabilized by the a⁰a⁰c⁻ octahedral rotations protected by nonsymmorphic symmetry. Upon interfacing with LaFeO₃, interface-induced lattice distortions lift the Dirac degeneracy.

Probing the Non collinear Magnetism in CoV2O4 using Spin hall Magnetoresistance

CoV₂O₄ hosts a rich magnetic phase diagram comprising paramagnetic, collinear ferrimagnetic, spin-reorientation, and noncollinear magnetic states. We probe the noncollinear magnetism using spin Hall magnetoresistance.
